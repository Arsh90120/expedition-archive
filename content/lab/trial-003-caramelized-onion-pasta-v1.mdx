## TRIAL PARAMETERS

Made this at camp. No kitchen, no rolling pin. The goal was fresh fettuccine from scratch with a caramelized onion cream sauce. June 20th, started the dough at 10am.

## EXECUTION LOG

- **1000** — Mixed dough by hand. Flour, eggs, olive oil, salt. Kneaded until it stopped fighting back.
- **1000–1700** — Let it rest seven hours. This was the right call.
- **1700** — Rolled it out with a water bottle. Actually worked.
- **1700** — Cut into fettuccine strips by hand. Uneven but close enough.
- **1700** — Two onions, butter, low heat. Left them alone for an hour.
- **1800** — Added more butter, heavy cream, pasta water. Tossed the pasta in.

## INCIDENT REPORT

- Too sweet. The onions took over and there was nothing to push back against them.
- No acid, no heat, nothing salty enough to balance it out.
- Water bottle rolling pin left some thick spots. Fine for camp, wouldn't do it in a real kitchen.

## FIELD VERDICT

The texture was genuinely good — fresh pasta cooked at a campsite with a water bottle. That part worked. The sauce was one note. Sweet the whole way through, and by the end of the bowl it was a lot.

v2: soy sauce when the onions are done, chili oil at the plate, maybe some lemon or a white wine deglaze before the cream goes in. The base recipe is solid. It just needs something to cut it.
