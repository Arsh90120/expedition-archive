## TRIAL PARAMETERS

Location: campsite. Equipment: one pot, one pan, a water bottle, and patience. Target: fresh fettuccine from scratch with a caramelized onion cream sauce. No kitchen. No rolling pin. Full commitment.

## EXECUTION LOG

- **1000** — Fresh pasta dough mixed by hand. Flour, eggs, olive oil, salt. Kneaded until smooth.
- **1000–1700** — Dough rested 7 hours. This was correct.
- **1700** — Rolled out using a water bottle. Worked better than expected. Cut into fettuccine strips by hand.
- **1700** — Two onions into the pan with butter. Caramelized low and slow for 1 hour. No shortcuts.
- **1800** — Added more butter, heavy cream, pasta water. Tossed the fresh pasta in. Plated at camp.

## INCIDENT REPORT

- Sauce ran too sweet — caramelized onions dominated the entire flavor profile without a counterbalance
- No acid, no heat, no umami to cut through the richness
- Water bottle rolling pin introduced minor thickness inconsistency — acceptable for field conditions

## FIELD VERDICT

The technique was sound. Fresh pasta in the field, seven-hour dough rest, an hour of caramelization — the execution was serious. The result was divine in texture and too sweet in flavor. This is a one-ingredient fix away from being elite. v2 protocol: soy sauce for umami depth, chili oil for heat, maybe a splash of white wine or lemon to cut the sweetness. The water bottle rolling pin stays — that's not a bug, that's the move.

## v2 TARGET ADJUSTMENTS

- Add soy sauce (1 tbsp) at onion finish for umami
- Chili oil at plate — not in sauce
- Consider small lemon squeeze or white wine deglaze before cream
- Parmesan if available in field
