## BRIEFING

Mount Monadnock. One of the most climbed mountains in the world — which means nothing when you're the one climbing it. Departed trailhead at 1000. Summit reached at 1200. Two hours up, conditions mild on ascent, brutal on the way back down.

## EQUIPMENT MANIFEST

- Day pack (standard load)
- Trail runners
- Water (enough — barely)
- Food (attempted)
- Layers for summit wind

## DEBRIEF

Ascent was straightforward. Scenic the whole way — open granite slabs near the top, views in every direction. Summit at 1200 felt earned. Wind at the top was serious — sustained gusts, couldn't get a proper meal in. Tried to eat, gave up, just stood there taking it in.

Decline was the unexpected part. Knees started feeling the grade on the way down, rocks were uneven, pace had to slow. Harder than the climb up in a different way — the kind of hard that teaches you something.

## INCIDENT REPORT

- Wind at summit was stronger than anticipated — eating was effectively impossible
- Decline significantly harsher than expected; descent technique needs work
- No serious gear failures, but layering could have been better planned for summit exposure

## FIELD VERDICT

This was the one. Not the hardest mission on record, not the most technical. But somewhere between the summit wind and the long way down, backpacking stopped being something I wanted to try and became something I needed to keep doing. Clearance Level 3 — earned clean.
