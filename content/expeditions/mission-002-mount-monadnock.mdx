## BRIEFING

Left the trailhead at 10. Summit by noon. I didn't know what I was getting into — I just knew I wanted to do it.

## EQUIPMENT MANIFEST

- Day pack
- Trail runners
- Water
- Food (attempted)
- One extra layer

## DEBRIEF

The climb up was fine. Open granite near the top, views everywhere, felt good the whole way. Got to the summit and the wind was stupid strong — not dangerous, just relentless. Tried to eat, gave up after two bites. Just stood there getting hit by gusts and looking out.

The way down was harder than expected. Knees started complaining around halfway. The rocks were uneven and my pace slowed way down. Nobody warns you about the descent.

## INCIDENT REPORT

- Brought food, ate none of it. Wind made it impossible.
- Decline was rough. Descent technique is something to actually work on.
- Layering was fine but could've had something heavier for the summit.

## FIELD VERDICT

This was the first time hiking felt like something I'd actually keep doing. Not sure what it was — maybe the wind, maybe standing on top of something and not having anyone hand it to you. Monadnock is supposed to be a beginner mountain. Didn't feel beginner on the way down.
